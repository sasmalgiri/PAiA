Frequently asked

Do I need to be online?

No — the core loop is fully offline. The only outbound calls are: to your local Ollama daemon (loopback); to download Whisper / Tesseract / Piper models on first use (one time, cached); and to GitHub Releases for auto-update (toggleable).

What cloud services do you run?

Only the billing webhook that issues signed license keys — it never sees your chats, prompts, or documents. Everything the product does runs on your device or talks directly to third parties you configured yourself.

How does the trial work?

14 days, no card, no signup. First launch records the start date locally. Every Pro feature unlocks for those 14 days. When it ends, you fall back to Free — every thread, document, memory, and artifact you created during the trial stays.

Can I extend my trial?

If someone shares a PAiA referral code with you (or we comp you one for a bug report), you can paste it into Settings → License → Redeem. Signed Ed25519 blobs, replay-protected.

Refunds?

30 days, no questions, full refund. Monthly plans cancel at the end of the current period. Yearly plans can cancel and refund pro-rata.

Commercial use?

Yes, Pro and Team both cover commercial use. Pro is per-seat (one person). Team is multi-seat with per-contract terms + an optional DPA.

How many machines per license?

Pro: activate on up to 3 of your own devices. Team: one seat per named user, activate on that user's primary and backup device. Transferable if you change machines.

How do you enforce licensing?

Ed25519 signed JSON blobs, verified offline in the app. No phone-home, no DRM, no anti-tampering. Honest customers pay; pirates aren't your customers.

Education discount?

Yes — 50% off Pro with any .edu email or verifiable student status. For classrooms on the Team tier, email us directly and we'll quote by enrollment.

What happens to my data if I cancel?

Nothing. Everything lives in your local user-data folder. Cancelling just locks Pro features. Your threads, documents, memory, and artifacts remain and work under the Free caps.

Is PAiA open source?

The redaction library is (MIT). The app itself is source-available for audit but proprietary — see the EULA. We plan to open more components over time.
